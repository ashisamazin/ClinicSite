Welcome

Welcome to the Diabetic Retinopathy Screening Service for Surrey (DRSSS) site. Our service has been running for around four years. We are one of the largest screening services in the country, with around 45,000 patients. We have a dedicated team of accredited screeners and admin staff who work towards keeping our servicer running at the highest standard.

What We Do

Diabetic retinopathy is one of the many complications of diabetes. As part of your diabetic care it is important to attend for annual screening. Diabetic retinopathy is a major cause of blindness, therefore, attending for annual screening is very important to help reduce your risk of sight loss. It affects the blood vessels in the back of your eye, the area known as your retina. As with many of the other diabetic complications, keeping your diabetes well controlled is essential, as this can have an effect on your eyes.

The screening process involves taking digital photographs of your retina, which can then be examined for any sign of diabetic retinopathy. The longer a patient has diabetes, the more likely there will be some effect on the eyes, however it will not always be sight threatening. It is important to make sure that if necessary, treatment is given at the right time. If there are any signs that vision may be at risk, we will refer patient to an Ophthalmologist, who will then decide if any treatment is needed.
